New Study: Digital Eye Strain Now Affects 71% of Extended Screen Users
Researchers at Stanford found that prolonged screen exposure exceeding four hours daily significantly increases eye strain risk, particularly among adults over 40 who use computers for work.
The study identified poor contrast settings and small font sizes as the two leading causes of visual fatigue in office environments, affecting productivity and long-term eye health.
Participants who used adaptive text enhancement tools reported a 38% reduction in reported eye discomfort after two weeks of regular use during their normal browsing sessions.
